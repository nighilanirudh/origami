Does the class size matter?

Yes, it certainly matters to US…

It’s the educational philosophy of ‘The Origami Concept School’, to design the class for small number of learners. Smaller the class size, the bigger the Opportunities!

We believe that the children learn at their best when they feel welcomed, valued, encouraged, engaged, comfortable and safe. All these are made possible with lesser students in a class and in the school. We envisage to have only 20-22 kids in a class, facilitated by 2 teachers in the classroom, restricted to only one section for every grade and in all there would be a big family of educators and learners in the campus. This ambience will allow the learners to know each and every other learner across the grades in the campus. They learn to work individually as well to work collaboratively. They connect more closely not only with their peers in class, but also with all at school which results in lasting friendships.

So how does this small-sized classroom matter in learning? Your child is heard more, noticed keen, offered support enough, and guided well. They enjoy the undivided attention of the two teachers in the classroom, and this creates the climate for learning. Giving a child a sense of who she/he is and where she/he fits in is part of our philosophy. Each child has a unique learning pattern, and we tailor make the teaching to suit each one of them. The educators cater to the individual learning need of every child, which makes the class more interactive and participative. Their learning gets thus enhanced and they gain confidence and esteem boosted. Educators diagnose and track the learners’ learning efficiently and provide feedback for their progressive development.

When we develop a love for learning in students, every day is enjoyable for them and there’s no looking back, we have already equipped them for a lifetime of achievements!
